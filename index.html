About Me

I'm a second-year Master of Public Policy student at UC Berkeley's Goldman School of Public Policy, where I specialize in AI governance and technology policy. My work sits at the intersection of investigative journalism, data analysis, and public policy.

Currently, I serve as a Research Analyst at TechEquity Collaborative, where I investigate Big Tech lobbying patterns and regulatory capture in California's AI governance landscape. My research has analyzed over $57 million in lobbying expenditures and examined opposition tactics to legislation like SB 1047, AB 1018, and SB 53.

Before graduate school, I spent five years as Senior Correspondent at ThePrint, India's largest digital news organization, where I published over 600 articles covering technology policy, algorithmic bias, and government accountability. I was awarded the International Press Institute Award in 2022 for my investigative reporting on COVID-19.

My journalism consistently focused on how technology systems impact marginalized communities—from exposing algorithmic bias in India's Swayam educational platform to investigating the edtech sector's accountability gaps.
